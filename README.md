SYNAPSE

Smart College Event & Collaboration Platform

Synapse is a student-focused platform designed to solve one of the biggest problems in college events and hackathons — finding the right people, building teams, managing collaboration, and discovering opportunities in one place.

Right now, most colleges depend on scattered WhatsApp groups, random Instagram posts, Google Forms, and word of mouth for events. Because of this, many students miss opportunities, fail to build teams, or never participate at all.

Synapse brings everything into one unified platform.


---

The Core Problem

In colleges, event participation and collaboration are highly unstructured.

Students face problems like:

Difficulty finding teammates

No proper platform to discover events

Communication scattered across multiple apps

Students with skills staying unnoticed

Last-minute team formation chaos

Many students unable to participate because they don’t have a team

Organizers struggling to manage registrations and engagement


A large number of talented students never participate simply because they cannot find the right team.

Synapse is built to fix that.


---

What is Synapse?

Synapse is an all-in-one college collaboration ecosystem that combines:

Event discovery

Smart team formation

Open recruitment system

Real-time collaboration

Community networking

Team management tools

AI-assisted features


It acts like a mix of LinkedIn, Discord, and Hackathon platforms — but designed specifically for college students.

Basically… if Tony Stark made a college event app after getting tired of WhatsApp groups.

Core Features

1. Auto Team Formation (Flagship Feature)

One of the most important and unique features of Synapse.

Students can enter:

Skills

Interests

Domain expertise

Preferred roles

Experience level

Availability


The system intelligently suggests or forms balanced teams based on compatibility.

Instead of randomly asking:

> “Bro anyone need one more member?”



students get matched with people who actually complement their skills.

Benefits

Faster team building

Balanced skill distribution

Better collaboration

Helps introverted students participate

Reduces last-minute panic

Increases event participation



---

2. Open Team Feature

Teams can publicly open recruitment for missing roles.

Example:

Team needs a UI/UX designer

Another team needs a backend developer

Someone needs a presentation speaker


Students can browse open positions and apply directly.

This solves the biggest college event issue:

> Incomplete teams.



Features Included

Role-based recruitment

Team requirement posts

Join requests

Team leader approvals

Skill matching

Visibility for solo participants


This feature makes collaboration dynamic instead of closed.
